Vito Saggese, Ph.D.

Astrophysics & Machine Learning Researcher

I am a researcher bridging the gap between astrophysics and artificial intelligence. My work focuses on discovering new worlds through exoplanet transits and gravitational microlensing, as well as applying advanced machine learning techniques to analyze the Martian surface. I am passionate about using data-driven approaches to unlock the mysteries of the universe.

Research Areas

Exoplanet Transits

Analyzing light curves to identify and characterize new planetary candidates in distant star systems.

Gravitational Microlensing

Detecting cold exoplanets and dark masses by observing the bending of light caused by gravitational fields.

Machine Learning for Mars

Applying deep neural networks and computer vision to classify morphological features on the Martian surface.
